Open-world Knowledge Discovery (OpenKD)

Colocated with the 37th Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems

About

An efficient functioning of modern society often relies on knowledge discovery (KD) techniques to harness the vast amounts of data generated every day in every sphere of life. For example, social media platforms (e.g., Facebook) leverage recommender systems to understand the human interactions and preferences of billions of users; healthcare applications rely on the proliferation of wearable devices and smart home appliances to analyze various aspects of human lives, including health metrics, environmental conditions, and energy consumption; scientific fields like astronomy use automated algorithms to discovery celestial phenomena from petabytes of data. Existing KD techniques like artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) models are often developed in a closed-world environment, where all test classes are available during the training phase. Despite the substantial progress in developing computational algorithms, models, and tools for knowledge discovery, the real-world environment that deploys KD techniques is dynamic, where new knowledge and new classes arrive regularly (e.g., a new event on social media platforms, a new virus that threats human health, new star in the universe). And existing techniques that follow closed-world assumptions cannot address these cases. Thus, it is crucial to study how to discover knowledge in an open-world setting. There are several key research questions involved in open-world knowledge discovery, including:

  • How to enable new knowledge accumulation and retention for KD/AI/ML techniques?
  • How to develop a reliable and trusted platform for open-world knowledge discovery?
  • How to leverage open-world knowledge discovery to benefit scientific domains and real-world applications?

This one-day workshop (OpenKD-NeurIPS'23) aims to bring together researchers and practitioners from different backgrounds to answer these research questions. The workshop will consist of contributed talks, contributed posters, invited talks, and discussion panels on a wide variety of methods and applications. Work-in-progress papers, demos, and visionary papers are also welcomed. We will also include invited papers for both oral presentations and poster presentations. This workshop intends to share visions of investigating new approaches and methods at the intersection of open-world knowledge discovery and real-world applications.

Call for Papers

Workshop Theme and Topics of Interests

We invite submissions on a broad range of open-world knowledge discoveries. We welcome many types of papers, such as (but are not limited to):

  • Research papers;
  • Work-in-progress papers;
  • Demo papers;
  • Visionary papers/white papers;
  • Appraisal papers of existing methods or toolboxes;
  • Evaluatory papers on assumptions, methods or toolboxes;
  • Relevant work that will be or has been published.

Topics of interests include (but are not limited to):

  • Incremental learning, online learning, continual learning, lifelong learning;
  • Out-of-distribution detection and generalization;
  • Transfer learning, domain adaption, domain generalization;
  • Information extraction;
  • Scientific knowledge discovery, artificial intelligence (AI) for science;
  • Trusted knowledge discovery and management (e.g., privacy, fairness, transparency);
  • Industry applications of open-world knowledge discovery;
  • Datasets and benchmarks for open-world knowledge discovery.
